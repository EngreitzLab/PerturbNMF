Synthesis

This gene expression program represents a **synaptic signaling module** that is paradoxically active during early iPSC-derived endothelial/mesoderm differentiation (D0), despite its clear neuronal functional identity. The program is enriched for genes involved in chemical synaptic transmission and postsynaptic signaling, with key components including **SHANK2**, **DLGAP1**, **ADCY2**, **SNTG2**, and **CADM2**. **SHANK2** and **DLGAP1** emerge as central nodes in this program, both functioning as postsynaptic scaffolding proteins within the PSD-95 complex. Evidence demonstrates that "distinct components of the PSD-95 protein complex differentially regulate learning, cognitive flexibility and reaction times in cognitive processing" (PMID:33347690). **SHANK2** specifically "identifies a subset of glycinergic neurons involved in altered nociception" and shows positive correlation with **Grin1** (PMID:37316943), while **DLGAP1** variants are associated with OCD-like phenotypes (PMID:39177810) and glutamatergic pathway dysfunction in major depressive disorder (PMID:35136251). **ADCY2**, highly expressed in the CNS (PMID:39073001), likely provides the cAMP signaling component essential for synaptic plasticity. **CADM2** contributes cell adhesion functions, with genome-wide association studies linking it to "risky behaviors and self-control" (PMID:33604983). The program's regulatory network includes positive regulators **SETDB1**, **CTR9**, and **PSMD4**, alongside negative regulators **G3BP1** and **DGCR8**. The presence of this neuronal synaptic program at D0 of endothelial/mesoderm differentiation suggests either: (1) transient expression of neuronal genes during early pluripotent transitions, or (2) shared developmental pathways between neural and mesodermal lineages involving synaptic signaling components.
