Synthesis

This gene expression program represents a **smooth muscle and cytoskeletal contractile apparatus** during early iPSC-derived endothelial/mesoderm differentiation at day 3. The program is characterized by coordinated expression of multiple actin isoforms and contractile proteins that form the foundation of cellular motility and structural organization. The core contractile machinery includes multiple actin isoforms - **ACTA1**, **ACTA2**, and **ACTC1** - which "comprises six different cell-type specific isoforms" essential for cellular function (PMID:33761369). **ACTA2** (smooth muscle α-actin) plays a particularly important role, as it "localizes to the nucleus in wildtype smooth muscle cells, enriches in the nucleus with SMC differentiation, and associates with chromatin remodeling complexes and SMC contractile gene promotors" (PMID:36909460). **TAGLN** and **ACTA2** work together as "the majority of Wnt-producing cells were contained in transgelin (Tagln+) and smooth muscle actin alpha2 (Acta2+) expressing populations" during tissue healing (PMID:34910127). The program includes structural proteins **KRT8** and **KRT18**, which function as "representative SBA-specific binding proteins" (PMID:37031286) and are upregulated together in vascular remodeling contexts (PMID:40624255). **SPP1** serves as both a diagnostic marker and mediator of "inflammatory, fibrotic, and impaired adaptive immune responses" through **SPP1-CD44** signaling (PMID:40623986). **ANKRD1** co-functions with **SPP1** as diagnostic biomarkers in tissue remodeling (PMID:34918678). Multiple studies confirm these genes cluster together in contractile gene networks, with **ACTA2**, **TAGLN**, and **TPM1** consistently co-expressed and "associated with overall or disease-free survival" in various contexts (PMID:37274283, PMID:38013282). This suggests the program represents an early mesenchymal differentiation state establishing contractile competency during endothelial-to-mesenchymal transition.
