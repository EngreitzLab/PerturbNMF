Synthesis

This gene expression program represents an early endothelial/mesoderm differentiation signature at day 3 of iPSC development, characterized by extracellular matrix organization and axon guidance pathways. The program is anchored by validated endoderm markers **EOMES** and **CER1**, which "are unique markers for specific cell fates: pluripotency (CNMD, NANOG, SPP1), endoderm (CER1, EOMES, GATA6), mesoderm (APLNR, HAND1, HOXB7)" (PMID:39358374). Notably, **EOMES** functions as both a positive regulator and top gene in this program, suggesting its central role in coordinating early lineage specification. The extracellular matrix component is prominently featured through **COL5A2**, which shows "increased expression of COL5A2, COL8A1, and COL12A1, encoding different collagen subunits, pivotal in cardiac extracellular matrix (ECM) structure" during cardiomyocyte development (PMID:35885988). This aligns with the program's enrichment for collagen biosynthesis and elastic fiber formation pathways. The axon guidance signature is represented by **NTN1**, described as "a laminin-related secreted protein involved in axon guidance and cell migration" that participates in "crucial biological processes and pathways related to cancer development and progression, including cell adhesion, axon guidance, immune response" (PMID:39863609). The regulatory network includes **SMAD3** and **ACVR1B** as positive regulators, consistent with TGF-β/BMP signaling's role in mesoderm specification, while negative regulators like **PLCG1** may fine-tune differentiation timing. The metabolic component **SERHL2** suggests coordinated lipid metabolism changes during this developmental transition (PMID:39657853). Together, this program captures the coordinated expression of structural, signaling, and guidance molecules essential for early cardiovascular lineage commitment.
