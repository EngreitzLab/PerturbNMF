## Synthesis

This gene expression program represents a **Rho GTPase-mediated endothelial migration and vascular development** module active during early iPSC-derived endothelial/mesoderm differentiation at day 3. The program is characterized by strong enrichment in Rho GTPase signaling, cell migration regulation, and axon guidance pathways.

**PLEKHG1** emerges as a central regulator in this program, functioning as a Rho guanine nucleotide exchange factor that "activates Cdc42 following activation by FYN" (PMID:35031323). Multiple studies confirm that **PLEKHG1** demonstrates "activity towards Cdc42" and is among the most efficient Cdc42 activators in primary human endothelial cells (PMID:31469028). This positions **PLEKHG1** as a key mediator of the program's GTPase regulatory functions, with clinical relevance as mutations cause "periventricular white matter abnormalities" (PMID:39202455).

The vascular components **FLT1** and **NRP1** provide endothelial-specific context to this program. **FLT1** shows consistent upregulation in endothelial cells across disease states, with "endothelial and microglial FLT1 expression upregulated in clinical AD patients" (PMID:39641382). **NRP1**, part of the VEGF signaling network, demonstrates protective associations where "higher astrocyte NRP1 is associated with lower tau density" (PMID:39641382).

The positive regulation by **BMPR1A**, **SMAD5**, and **SMAD1** suggests BMP/TGF-β pathway integration with Rho GTPase signaling during endothelial differentiation. Negative regulation by **NOTCH1** and **TGFBR2** indicates tight control mechanisms preventing excessive activation. This program likely coordinates early vascular development through Rho GTPase-mediated cytoskeletal reorganization and migration, with **PLEKHG1**-CDC42 signaling as a central regulatory axis.
